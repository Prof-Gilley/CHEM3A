11.1.3 Stoichiometry and the Ideal Gas Law

📋 COR Alignment

Gases | Gas Laws | PV = nRT applications in stoichiometry

Learning Objectives

  • Convert between gas properties (P, V, T) and moles using PV = nRT
  • Apply the ideal gas law to stoichiometric calculations
  • Solve problems involving gaseous reactants and products

📹 itsDrDan Video Resources

Key Concepts

  • PV = nRT where R = 0.08206 L·atm/(mol·K)
  • Always convert temperature to Kelvin
  • Check units carefully when using the ideal gas law

11.1.4 Gas Stoichiometry at STP

📋 COR Alignment

Gases at STP | Avogadro's Law | Molar volume of gases

Learning Objectives

  • Define standard temperature and pressure (STP)
  • Apply the molar volume of a gas at STP (22.4 L/mol)
  • Use STP conditions to simplify gas stoichiometry calculations

📹 itsDrDan Video Resources

Key Concepts

  • STP: T = 273 K (0°C) and P = 1 atm
  • At STP: 1 mole of any gas = 22.4 L
  • This simplifies gas calculations significantly

11.2 Limiting Reactant

📋 COR Alignment

Limiting Reactant Calculations | Identifying excess reagents

Learning Objectives

  • Identify the limiting reactant in a chemical reaction
  • Calculate the theoretical yield based on the limiting reactant
  • Determine the amount of excess reactant remaining

📹 itsDrDan Video Resources

Key Concepts

  • The limiting reactant is completely consumed first
  • The limiting reactant determines the maximum product yield
  • Compare mole ratios to identify limiting reactant
  • Calculate using the "smaller amount method" or "comparison method"

11.3 Theoretical Yield and Percent Yield

📋 COR Alignment

Percent yield calculations | Comparing theoretical vs actual yields

Learning Objectives

  • Calculate theoretical yield from stoichiometry
  • Determine percent yield from actual and theoretical yields
  • Understand why actual yield is often less than theoretical yield

📹 itsDrDan Video Resources

Key Concepts

  • Theoretical yield = maximum possible from stoichiometry
  • Actual yield = amount actually obtained in lab
  • Percent yield = (Actual yield / Theoretical yield) × 100%
  • Percent yield is always ≤ 100% for real reactions

📹 itsDrDan Video Resources

Key Concepts

Comprehensive practice incorporating all stoichiometry concepts

Practice Problem Categories

  • General Stoichiometry: Mole ratio and conversion factor problems
  • Mass-Mass Calculations: Converting between masses of different substances
  • Solution Stoichiometry: Problems involving molarity and volume
  • Gas Stoichiometry: STP and ideal gas law problems
  • Limiting Reactant: Identifying limiting reactants and excess amounts

Problem-Solving Strategy

  • Always start by writing a balanced equation
  • Identify what you're given and what you need to find
  • Set up a clear conversion pathway through moles
  • Check units at each step
  • Verify your answer makes sense
