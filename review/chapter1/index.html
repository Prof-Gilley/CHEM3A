üéØ 3. Significant Figures - Precision in Measurements

What are Significant Figures?

Significant Figures (sig figs) are the digits in a measurement that carry meaningful information about its precision. They indicate the reliability and accuracy of a measured or calculated value.

Rules for Identifying Significant Figures:

  • All non-zero digits are significant (123 has 3 sig figs)
  • Zeros between non-zero digits are significant (1005 has 4 sig figs)
  • Leading zeros are NOT significant (0.0025 has 2 sig figs)
  • Trailing zeros after decimal are significant (2.500 has 4 sig figs)
  • Trailing zeros without decimal are ambiguous (use scientific notation!)
Sig Figs in Calculations:

Multiplication/Division:
Result has same sig figs as measurement with FEWEST sig figs
Example: 2.5 √ó 3.42 = 8.6 (not 8.55)

Addition/Subtraction:
Result has same decimal places as measurement with FEWEST decimal places
Example: 12.11 + 18.0 = 30.1 (not 30.11)

Solution:

  1. Perform calculation: 4.56 √ó 1.4 = 6.384
  2. 4.56 has 3 sig figs, 1.4 has 2 sig figs
  3. Use fewest (2 sig figs)
  4. Answer: 6.4 m¬≤

üîÑ 4. Unit Conversions & Dimensional Analysis

What is Dimensional Analysis?

Dimensional Analysis (also called the factor-label method or unit-factor method) is a problem-solving technique that uses conversion factors to change from one unit to another. The key is that units cancel algebraically just like numbers.

Steps for Dimensional Analysis:

  • Step 1: Identify the given quantity and desired unit
  • Step 2: Find conversion factor(s) relating the units
  • Step 3: Set up so unwanted units cancel
  • Step 4: Multiply across numerators, divide by denominators
  • Step 5: Check that units make sense and round appropriately
Conversion Factor Approach:

Given √ó (Conversion Factor) = Desired

Example: Convert 5.0 km to meters
5.0 km √ó (1000 m / 1 km) = 5000 m
(Note: km cancels, leaving m)

What is Density?

Density is the ratio of an object's mass to its volume. It is an intensive property (independent of amount) that helps identify substances and predict whether objects will float or sink.

Key Density Concepts:

  • Formula: Density = Mass / Volume (D = m/V)
  • Common Units: g/cm¬≥, g/mL, kg/m¬≥
