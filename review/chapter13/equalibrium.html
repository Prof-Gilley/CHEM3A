Le Chatelier's Principle

  • Principle: When a stress is applied to a system at equilibrium, the system shifts to relieve the stress
  • Concentration: Add reactant/remove product ‚Üí shift RIGHT; Add product/remove reactant ‚Üí shift LEFT
  • Temperature: Endothermic (heat = reactant): ‚ÜëT shifts RIGHT; Exothermic (heat = product): ‚ÜëT shifts LEFT
  • Pressure: ‚ÜëP shifts toward fewer moles of gas; ‚ÜìP shifts toward more moles of gas

Example: N‚ÇÇ(g) + 3H‚ÇÇ(g) ‚áå 2NH‚ÇÉ(g) + heat

Add N‚ÇÇ: Shifts RIGHT ‚Üí more NH‚ÇÉ produced

Remove NH‚ÇÉ: Shifts RIGHT ‚Üí more NH‚ÇÉ produced

Increase T: Shifts LEFT (exothermic, heat is product)

Increase P: Shifts RIGHT (4 moles ‚Üí 2 moles)

13.4.1 Effect of Concentration Changes

Learning Objectives

  • Predict equilibrium shifts when reactants are added or removed
  • Predict equilibrium shifts when products are added or removed
  • Understand that K value remains constant with concentration changes

General Rules

  • Add reactant ‚Üí equilibrium shifts RIGHT (toward products)
  • Remove reactant ‚Üí equilibrium shifts LEFT (toward reactants)
  • Add product ‚Üí equilibrium shifts LEFT (toward reactants)
  • Remove product ‚Üí equilibrium shifts RIGHT (toward products)
  • The system responds to minimize the disturbance

Industrial Application: Haber-Bosch Process

N‚ÇÇ(g) + 3H‚ÇÇ(g) ‚áå 2NH‚ÇÉ(g)

NH‚ÇÉ is continuously removed ‚Üí equilibrium shifts RIGHT ‚Üí maximizes ammonia production!

13.4.2 Temperature and Pressure Effects

Learning Objectives

  • Distinguish between endothermic and exothermic reactions
  • Predict temperature effects on equilibrium position
  • Apply pressure changes to gas-phase equilibria
  • Understand when pressure changes have no effect

Temperature Effects

  • Endothermic (ŒîH > 0): heat + reactants ‚áå products
  • ‚ÜëT shifts RIGHT (adding heat = adding reactant)
  • ‚ÜìT shifts LEFT
  • Exothermic (ŒîH < 0): reactants ‚áå products + heat
  • ‚ÜëT shifts LEFT (adding heat = adding product)
  • ‚ÜìT shifts RIGHT
  • Important: Temperature changes actually change K value!

Pressure Effects (GAS-PHASE ONLY!)

  • Increase pressure ‚Üí shifts toward FEWER moles of gas
  • Decrease pressure ‚Üí shifts toward MORE moles of gas
  • Equal moles on both sides ‚Üí NO SHIFT
  • Liquids and solids are incompressible - pressure doesn't affect them

Example: 2NO‚ÇÇ(g) ‚áå N‚ÇÇO‚ÇÑ(g)

2 moles gas ‚Üí 1 mole gas
