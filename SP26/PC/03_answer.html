üìè Sig Fig Counting Rules Summary

  • Trapped zeros ‚Üí Always count (zeros between non-zero digits)

Question 2
A student records the mass of a sample as 0.004050 kg. Which digits in this measurement are significant, and why are the leading zeros not counted?
Answer
4 significant figures: 4, 0, 5, 0
üí° Explanation

In 0.004050 kg:

  • 0.00 ‚Äî The leading zeros are placeholders that show where the decimal point goes. They don't represent measured precision.
  • 4050 ‚Äî These four digits are significant:
    • 4 ‚Äî First non-zero digit, always significant
    • 0 ‚Äî Trapped zero between 4 and 5, always counts
    • 5 ‚Äî Non-zero digit, always significant
    • 0 ‚Äî Trailing zero after decimal, shows precision

Why don't leading zeros count? They only indicate the magnitude of the number‚Äîthey tell us where to place the decimal point, not how precisely the measurement was made. If we converted to grams (4.050 g), the leading zeros disappear but the precision stays the same!

Question 3 (Multiple Choice)
A nurse measures out 250. mL of saline solution. The decimal point after the zero indicates that this measurement has:
(A) 2 significant figures
‚úì (B) 3 significant figures
(C) 4 significant figures
(D) infinite significant figures
Correct Answer
B ‚Äî 3 significant figures
üí° Why the Decimal Point Matters

The decimal point after 250. is intentional! It tells us that the trailing zero is significant.

  • 250 mL (no decimal) ‚Üí 2 sig figs (ambiguous trailing zeros)
  • 250. mL (with decimal) ‚Üí 3 sig figs (the zero is measured, not estimated)

This is a common notation in scientific and medical settings to clearly communicate precision.

üìê The Addition/Subtraction Rule

‚ö†Ô∏è This is DIFFERENT from the mult/div rule! Here we count decimal places, not total sig figs.

Part 4: Combined Operations & Exact Numbers

Question 8
Calculate the following:
(25.0 g + 3.200 g) √∑ 12.5 mL
Answer
2.26 g/mL
üí° Step-by-Step Solution (Order of Operations!)
1 First, do the addition (in parentheses):
‚Ä¢ 25.0 g ‚Üí 1 decimal place (limiting)
‚Ä¢ 3.200 g ‚Üí 3 decimal places
‚Ä¢ Sum: 25.0 + 3.200 = 28.200 ‚Üí Round to 28.2 g
2 Then, do the division:
‚Ä¢ 28.2 g ‚Üí 3 sig figs
‚Ä¢ 12.5 mL ‚Üí 3 sig figs
‚Ä¢ 28.2 √∑ 12.5 = 2.256
3 Round to 3 sig figs:
2.256 ‚Üí 2.26 g/mL
üî¢ Order of Operations with Sig Figs
  1. Follow PEMDAS (Parentheses first!)
  2. Apply the appropriate sig fig rule at EACH step
  3. Addition/Subtraction ‚Üí decimal places
  4. Multiplication/Division ‚Üí sig figs
Question 9
A pharmacist counts exactly 30 tablets, each with a mass of 0.325 g. What is the total mass of all the tablets, reported with the correct number of significant figures? Explain why the number 30 does not limit your answer.
Answer
9.75 g
üí° Step-by-Step Solution
1 Identify the values:
‚Ä¢ 30 tablets ‚Üí Exact number (counted) ‚Üí Infinite sig figs!
‚Ä¢ 0.325 g ‚Üí 3 sig figs ‚Üê (this limits the answer)
2 Calculate total mass:
30 √ó 0.325 g = 9.75 g
3 Determine sig figs:
Since 30 is exact, we use 3 sig figs from 0.325 g.
Answer: 9.75 g (3 sig figs)
Why doesn't "30" limit the answer?

The number 30 is an exact number because the pharmacist counted the tablets‚Äîthey didn't measure them. There are exactly 30 tablets, not approximately 30. You could write it as 30.000000... with infinite precision.

Other examples of exact numbers:
  • 12 eggs in a dozen
  • 2.54 cm in 1 inch (by definition)
  • 100 cm in 1 m (by definition)
  • The "2" in 2œÄr
‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistake from PC-03

Some students wrote 10 g, thinking "30" has only 1 or 2 sig figs. But counted numbers don't limit precision!

üìã Key Takeaways for SC-T0

  • Multiplication/Division: Round to FEWEST SIG FIGS
  • Addition/Subtraction: Round to FEWEST DECIMAL PLACES
  • Trailing zeros after a decimal ARE significant (0.030 has 2 sig figs)
  • Exact numbers (counted items, defined conversions) have INFINITE sig figs
  • Scientific notation: count only the coefficient digits
  • Follow PEMDAS, applying the appropriate rule at each step
  • When in doubt, ask: "Which measurement is least precise?"
